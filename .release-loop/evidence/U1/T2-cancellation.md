# T2-cancellation — batch-end embedding

- Plan: `docs/superpowers/plans/2026-07-19-v0.15.0-self-archaeology-blame-plan.md`, row T2
- Source commit: `94d48d47db42a505115fda5bd2fb2618acd589cd`

This cell's proof is analytical, per the brief (U1-brief.md line 67) and the plan text (line 204: "Cancellation: abort during embed leaves confirmed decisions intact; embeddings regenerate on next confirm or `ec index` run."). No command output is fabricated for this cell.

Reasoning:
- The batch-end embedding block runs strictly *after* the per-candidate confirm loop has already completed and committed every confirmation (each `confirm_candidate` call is its own independently-committed CAS-claim + `BEGIN IMMEDIATE` promotion, per T1-success/T1-rollback). By the time `generate_embeddings(conn, repo_path, decisions_only=True)` is reached, every candidate in `result["confirmed"]` already has a durable `decisions` row and `promoted_decision_id` back-pointer — nothing about embedding generation can undo or block on those promotions.
- An abort (`KeyboardInterrupt` or process kill) delivered *during* the embedding call interrupts `generate_embeddings` itself, not the confirmations that already landed. Embeddings are additive, derived rows keyed off `decisions` — `generate_embeddings` is a scan-and-fill pass (per `core/embedding.py`) with no transactional coupling to the confirm loop, so a partial or zero-progress embedding pass leaves the already-confirmed decisions exactly as they were: durable, just possibly still missing vectors.
- Whether the interruption is a genuine `KeyboardInterrupt` or a raised exception from within `generate_embeddings`, the two are indistinguishable at the `try/except: pass` boundary from the confirmed-decisions' perspective — both leave the batch-end embedding block incomplete without touching `decision_candidates` or `decisions` state. `KeyboardInterrupt` is not `Exception` in Python's exception hierarchy, so a genuine keyboard interrupt during `generate_embeddings` would actually propagate past the `try/except Exception: pass` and out of `confirm_candidates_batch` rather than being swallowed — but the observable effect on durability is the same either way: confirmed decisions are untouched, and only the embedding pass is abandoned mid-flight.
- Resume semantics: because `decisions_only=True` scans for decisions still missing embeddings rather than tracking batch-scoped progress, re-running `confirm_candidates_batch` on a later batch (any invocation that confirms ≥1 candidate), or running `ec index`, naturally regenerates the missing embeddings for decisions left over from an aborted embedding pass — no special resume logic needed, matching the plan text verbatim.

Verification pointer: the T2-forced-failure evidence record is the nearest executable proof — an unconditional `RuntimeError` raised from inside `generate_embeddings` is, at the process-durability level, equivalent to an abort mid-embed: in both cases the embedding call does not complete, and the test proves `result["confirmed"]` and `result["failed"]` are unaffected, no exception propagates, and no confirmed candidate is reverted. The T2-rerun evidence record proves the regeneration-on-next-call half of this claim executably (a second confirming batch call triggers its own independent embedding call). The "genuine `KeyboardInterrupt` bypasses the `except Exception` swallow" nuance above is inherited from Python's exception hierarchy and the plan's design, not re-verified with a live interrupt injection, since simulating a real signal delivery mid-call is out of scope for a unit test.
